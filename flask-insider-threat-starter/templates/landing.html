03 / CYBER THREAT STORYTELLING

How Insider
Threats Emerge

Unauthorized Access

Internal network entry via insecure local connection points, or unauthorized secondary active sessions initiated during off-hours.

Privilege Abuse

Elevating execution permissions to gain access to locked databases or administrative network scopes without manager authorization logs.

Credential Misuse

Utilizing system credentials across atypical workspace segments or abnormal endpoints, flagging suspicious access session overlap.

Suspicious Downloads

Unusual bulk download volume thresholds crossed on internal documents, source files, or customer lists within a condensed timeline.

Data Exfiltration

Moving database chunks and sensitive logs to external drives or unregistered cloud drives, triggering automated security containment.
